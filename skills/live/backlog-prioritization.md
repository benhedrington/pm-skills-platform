## Summary
Use RICE, MoSCoW, or stack ranking to order work so the team always knows what to pull next.

## When to use this skill
- Your backlog is >2 weeks of work.
- Stakeholders disagree on what matters most.
- Sprint planning takes too long because priorities are unclear.

## The playbook
1. **List candidates** — everything proposed for the next planning horizon.
2. **Choose a framework**:
   - RICE for product features (Reach, Impact, Confidence, Effort).
   - MoSCoW for project deadlines.
   - Stack rank for small lists (<10 items).
3. **Score / sort** with the team. Do not do this alone.
4. **Cut the line** — draw a line. Below the line is "not now," not "never."
5. **Revisit weekly** — priorities decay faster than you think.

## Common anti-patterns
- Perfecting the score. Good enough today beats perfect next week.
- Prioritizing in a vacuum without engineering input on effort.
- Treating the ranked list as a promise rather than a plan.

## Exit criteria
- You can explain in one sentence why the top 3 items are top 3.
- The team can pull the next item without asking you.
