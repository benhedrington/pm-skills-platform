## Summary
Build a roadmap that communicates direction without overpromising delivery dates.

## When to use this skill
- Stakeholders ask "when will feature X ship?"
- The team needs a north star for the next quarter.
- You want to separate "committed" from "aspirational."

## The playbook
1. **Choose a horizon model** — Now / Next / Later or quarterly buckets.
2. **Theme over feature list** — group work by outcome, not ticket titles.
3. **Label confidence** — committed, planned, or exploratory.
4. **Share early and often** — a roadmap is a conversation, not a poster.
5. **Review monthly** — retire themes that no longer matter.

## Common anti-patterns
- Treating the roadmap as a Gantt chart with hard dates.
- Listing every minor feature to appease stakeholders.
- Updating it only once per quarter.

## Exit criteria
- Anyone in the company can read the roadmap and understand the top 3 bets.
- Engineering can use it to challenge scope without feeling micromanaged.
